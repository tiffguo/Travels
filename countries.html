London

London is the capital of England and the United Kingdom. It is among the oldest of the world’s great cities—its history spanning nearly two millennia—and one of the most cosmopolitan. London has historically undergone many victories and tragedies, and you can see it reflected in its rich cultural landmarks. Check out Tower Bridge, Westminster Abbey, Buckingham Palace, and the famed Big Ben. Go luxury shopping at Harrods and Selfridges, and don't forget to "mind the gap" on the tube!

Santorini

Cousteau looked for the lost city of Atlantis here! Santorini, the precious gem of the Aegean, is actually a group of islands in the southernmost part of the Cyclades. The Santorini islands are still an active volcano and the only volcano in the world whose crater is in the sea. Santorini is considered to be the most sought after place for a romantic getaway in Greece and has a growing reputation as a “wedding destination” for couples from all over the world. Hike from Oia to Fira, and indulge in Santorini's ultimate gastronomic experiences.

Amalfi

Amalfi is a town in a dramatic natural setting below steep cliffs on Italy’s southwest coast. Between the 9th and 11th centuries, it was the seat of a powerful maritime republic. The Arab-Norman Sant'Andrea cathedral at the heart of town, with its striped Byzantine facade, survives from this era. Looking around town and the surrounding hills, you'll quickly see that the primary product of the area is enormous orbs of lemons, picked fresh to make limoncello liqueur and used in local dishes, as well. The lemon motif shows up in regional ceramics and citrus scent fills the air as you meander about.

Copenhagen

Copenhagen is the capital of Denmark and is big enough to be a metropolis with shopping, culture and nightlife par excellence, yet still small enough to be intimate, safe and easy to navigate. Overlooking the Øresund strait with Sweden just minutes away, it is a cultural and geographic link between mainland Europe and Scandinavia. This is where old fairy tales blend with flashy new architecture and world-class design; where warm jazz mixes with cold electronica from Copenhagen's basements. Explore the iconic Nyhavn, Tivoli Gardens, and hipster neighborhoods!

Lisbon

Lisbon is Portugal’s hilly, coastal capital city. From imposing São Jorge Castle, the view encompasses the old city’s pastel-colored buildings, Tagus Estuary and Ponte 25 de Abril suspension bridge. Nearby, the National Azulejo Museum displays 5 centuries of decorative ceramic tiles. This beautiful city is blessed with buzzing nightlife (check out Pink Street!), take a tile painting class, eat a pastel de nata, and check out the world's second biggest aquarium.

Paros

Paros is a Greek island in the Aegean Sea best known for its beaches and traditional villages. Parikia, the bayside capital, is a boating and transportation hub for the Cyclades region. It's also home to the Panagia Ekatontapiliani, a landmark Byzantine church from the 4th century A.D. The north coast's Kolimbithres is a beach where naturally sculpted rocks form swimming coves. Be sure to browse the mythology imbued evil eye jewelry and take in the white-and-blue colored landscape!

Florence

Florence, capital of Italy’s Tuscany region, is home to many masterpieces of Renaissance art and architecture. One of its most iconic sights is the Duomo, a cathedral with a terracotta-tiled dome engineered by Brunelleschi and a bell tower by Giotto. The Galleria dell'Accademia displays Michelangelo’s “David” sculpture. The Uffizi Gallery exhibits Botticelli’s “The Birth of Venus” and da Vinci’s “Annunciation.”

Bath

Bath is the largest city in the county of Somerset, England, known for and named after its Roman-built baths. Built for pleasure and relaxation, beautiful Bath has been a wellbeing destination since Roman times. The waters are still a big draw, both at the ancient Roman Baths and the thoroughly modern Thermae Bath Spa, which houses the only natural thermal hot springs in Britain you can bathe in.

Cancun

Cancún, a Mexican city on the Yucatán Peninsula bordering the Caribbean Sea, is known for its beaches, numerous resorts and nightlife. t is at once a world-renowned vacation resort, an adventure base for exploring lagoons and caves and an open-air museum showcasing the ruined temples of the Mayans. I went here for my spring break trip my senior year of high school and made some incredible memories with my friends before we parted.

Beijing

Beijing, China’s sprawling capital, has history stretching back 3 millennia. Yet it’s known as much for modern architecture as its ancient sites such as the grand Forbidden City complex, the imperial palace during the Ming and Qing dynasties. Nearby, the massive Tiananmen Square pedestrian plaza is the site of Mao Zedong’s mausoleum and the National Museum of China, displaying a vast collection of cultural relics. I came here as part of an immersion program to understand my Chinese heritage, and the experience was unforgettable.

GuangZhou

My parents are from Guangzhou, so this is a city that is also dear to me! Guangzhou is a sprawling port city northwest of Hong Kong on the Pearl River. The city features avant-garde architecture such as Zaha Hadid’s Guangzhou Opera House (known as the “double pebble”); the carved box-shaped Guangdong Museum; and the iconic Canton TV Tower skyscraper, resembling a thin hourglass. The Chen Clan Ancestral Hall, a temple complex from 1894, also houses the Guangdong Folk Arts Museum.
